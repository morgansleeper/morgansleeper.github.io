Teaching

Teaching is one of the most rewarding parts of scholarship for me; I love being in the classroom, and trying to make my classes as engaging as possible for my students. I was fortunate enough to TA these classes during my time at UCSB, either as a Graduate Student Teaching Assistant or as a Head TA, both with weekly sections:

2016-2017

  • “Language, Power, and Learning” (LING/CHST 187)
  • “Introduction to Syntax” (LING 109)
  • “Introduction to Phonetics” (LING 106)

    • 2014-2015

  • “Language, Power, and Learning” (LING/CHST/EDUC 187)
  • “Language in Society” (LING 70)
  • “Introduction to Linguistics” (LING 20)

    • I also co-taught (with Kayla Palakurthy) a twice-weekly class on sociolinguistic justice at San Marcos High School in 2016, as part of the SKILLS program. Our students put together original research projects and came to UCSB to present them (in a poster session and in conference-style presentations) at the end of the course, and received college credit through Santa Barbara City College.
